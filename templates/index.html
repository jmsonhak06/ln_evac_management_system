ABOUT US

HISTORY

The Caraga Extension Program—ENGAGE Caraga is a community extension program implemented by the Department of Geodetic Engineering and College of Engineering and Geosciences & Caraga Center for Geo-Informatics (CCGeo). The program is based on the two (2) major projects on flood hazard mapping of the Caraga Region: Geo-informatics for the Systematic Assessment of Flood Effects and Risks for a Resilient Mindanao (GeoSAFER Agusan, 2014-2017), and the Philippine Light Detection and Ranging 1 Program (Phil-LiDAR 1, 2017- 2019).

Phil-LIDAR 1 Program, an expansion of the DREAM Program, aims to produce 3D flood and hazard maps for the 2/3 of the Philippine river systems. Aside from addressing disaster risk reduction and climate change adaptation, the resource information to be generated from this project will also be useful in providing the information requirements of various sectors in the country. On the other hand, the Geo-SAFER Agusan project was being implemented by the Caraga State University (CSU) through the Caraga Center for Geo-informatics (CCGeo) and is a component project funded by the DOST-PCIEERD. The project aims to conduct geo-informatics based approaches for the systematic assessment of flooding in the Agusan River Basin.

These two major projects share a common goal: to prepare LGUs for impending flood hazards. These projects originates the birth of the extension program—ENGAGE Caraga. This extension program sought to answer the questions:  How successful were the two projects in preparing the LGUs for flood hazards?  What are the impacts of the flood hazard maps and other products provided to the LGUs?  How can we further use the maps in building resilience and reducing vulnerability to flood hazards beyond flood hazard mapping?

OBJECTIVES

Primary Objective: To engage Caraga LGUs on the use of GeoSAFER Hazard Maps and other project outputs for building resilience and reducing vulnerability to flood hazards.

Specific Objectives:

1. To determine the impacts of two major R&D projects conducted by Caraga State University for flood hazard assessment of Caraga Region, namely Phil-LiDAR 1 and Geo-SAFER Agusan, to the flood hazard preparedness of Caraga Region LGU's;

2. The program also aims to identify the specific needs of LGUs in terms of building flood hazard resilience and reducing vulnerability;

3. To enhance and update the existing flood hazard maps;

4. To provide additional products and services to address the specific needs of the LGUs based on the assessment.

PROJECT TITLE: Flood Hazard Map Enhancement and Generation of Additional Products and Services

PROJECT OBJECTIVE

Primary Goal: To enhance existing flood hazard maps and to generate additional products and services to address the specific needs of LGUs.

Specific Objectives:

1. To enhance the methodology of flood hazard mapping through updating of geospatial datasets;

2. To procure and deploy sensors for flood monitoring and forecasting; and

3. To generate suitability modeling and mapping analyses for flood control structures and evacuation sites.
